RESEARCH EXPERIENCE
Thesis: Can training a multi-layer neural network with genetic algorithm outperform backpropagation algorithm in image classification?

The project was carried out to uncover the answer to the hypothesis of whether a network constructed with a Genetic algorithm (GA) can perform better than a network constructed with a Back-propagation algorithm (BP) in image classification problem. In the search for the answer, various literatures were reviewed and critically analyzed. Although a significant number of researchers were in favor of the GA in their research, a lack of step-by-step guidance on designing their neural networks and the learning algorithms results in lack of confidence for the reader to draw a clear conclusion on GA superiority over the BP in image classification.

To test out the hypothesis practically, a program was designed and various experiments were conducted to examine and analyze the dominance of the GA and the BP algorithm over each other in recognition problem. The research mainly focused on:

  • The methodology and conclusions of other researchers in the area of BP and GA algorithms performance with number of labeled dataset in data classification such as breast cancer, prime diabetes, yeast, Boston housing before conducting and modifying my own experiments
  • Obtaining, organizing and storing secondary dataset
  • Performing image processing and data normalization operation such as image smoothing, image binarization, image cropping and image scaling
  • Software development in Java and hardware set-up to perform experiments phases
  • Network construction, training and testing datasets with BP and GA algorithms

The research was applied in designing the experiments conducted, and results were compared and evaluated.
